# Craft & Distinctiveness Standard

Apply this whenever you produce or review any visual design. The bar: the result must
not look AI-generated, should feel engaging (immersive where it fits), and must be
visually excellent without distracting from the mission.

## The governing rule
**Beauty serves the mission.** Every distinctive or beautiful choice must earn its
place by helping the user do or feel what the product is for. If a choice only
impresses, it is decoration — cut it or dial it back.

## Avoid these AI-generated tells
- Default fonts untouched (Inter/Roboto/system) as the whole identity.
- Canned looks: beige+serif "editorial", purple/violet glow gradient, stark
  black-on-white broadsheet, generic rounded-card SaaS template.
- Centered/symmetric everything with no focal point or hierarchy.
- Grid of identical rounded cards with the same shadow.
- Decoration for its own sake (gradients everywhere, glassmorphism, emoji bullets).
- Filler copy ("elevate", "seamlessly", "unlock the power of", vague buttons).
- Stock-photo / generic-illustration vibe; no custom identity.
- No signature element; nothing memorable.
- Rhythmless spacing, mismatched radii, random accents.

## Produce crafted instead
- **One signature element** the product is "the one with" — everything else stays quiet.
- A real typeface + considered type ramp, used consistently.
- Intentional hierarchy: a clear focal point, deliberate emphasis, asymmetry where the
  surface allows.
- Cohesion: illustration, icons, motion, copy share one DNA.
- Specific, human copy in the product's own voice — including empty states and errors.
- Details that reward attention: transitions, edge cases, real empty/loading states.
- Bespoke over generic (custom illustration/iconography) where it fits.

## Premium = restraint + precision
Premium is the absence of anything cheap, not added luxury. It reads through precise
alignment and strict spacing rhythm, restraint (fewer elements, more space, one accent),
real materials, smooth motion (jank = cheap), tactile feedback, finish in overlooked
moments, and performance. Avoid false-premium costume: gold-on-black, forced dark mode,
gratuitous gradients/glass, decorative serifs, show-off animation.

Two temperatures: **sleek & polished** (SaaS/fintech/data — precision, speed, quiet
confidence) vs **warm & crafted** (consumer/family/wellness — bespoke illustration,
warm palette, cozy spacing, gentle motion). Both share the universal markers; they
differ only in temperature.

## Calibrate by surface
- **Marketing / portfolio** — lean into impact: cinematic hero, signature interaction,
  confident motion. High ceiling.
- **Consumer app** — immersion via custom illustration, character, atmosphere, delight;
  warm and high-craft, serving the emotional objective.
- **Regulated / operational dashboard** — NOT cinematic. Crafted = precise, calm, fast,
  trustworthy, dense done right (Linear/Stripe). No decorative flourishes.
- **Watch** — one perfect legible glance in the safe area, dark and calm. Near-total
  restraint.

## Critique step (run before shipping)
Ask explicitly: *"Does this read as crafted or AI-generated — which tells is it
showing?"* and *"Does every beautiful choice serve the mission, or is something showing
off?"* Use "beauty serves the mission" as the tie-breaker. Then fix what fails.
